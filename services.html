LIBRARY SERVICES

SERVICES OFFERED BY THE LIBRARY

Reference Service

Collections in this area are books used for additional references for its courses written by foreign author and about foreign information.



The JRMSU System Library:

1) shall provide information in the form of short answers to specific questions and guidance in locating materials for clients who appear in person, call on the telephone, or request information through written or electronic correspondence and social networking sites;

2) shall provide bibliographic verification to identify the correct author, title and publication data of items when appropriate;

3) may refer library users to other agencies and libraries in pursuit of needed information;
4) may use not only the Library's resources in printed form, but consult appropriate digital resources as well as the e-resources of "ready reference" information;

5) may use its website to publish topical guides on a variety of subjects and may include on its website links to other Internet resources deemed by the library staff to be potentially helpful to library users.

The library offers reference service to the faculty, staff, students, and other users. A reference services in-charge provides assistance in locating books and also in the Reserve Collections. He/she will also help in directing users to other sources when the information is not available and answer specific questions.
Material Booking and Reserve

This is the place wherein books are being reserved as required reading for their subjects. These books are usually limited in copies. To facilitate wide use and to insure equal opportunities among classmates, materials are loaned out for one hour within this area.



Only one book at a time may be borrowed from the reserve.



Reserve lists should be given to the circulation section by the faculty a week before classes start to give the librarian time to collect the materials and also, if the choices are not available in the library, to give the librarian time to look for substitute books.



Reservation covers only one semester/summer. If the instructor wishes to extend for the time span he/she must inform the librarian otherwise, the books will be returned to the shelf.
Inter System Library Loan

Interlibrary loans are due on the date indicated by the lending library of the JRMSU System.



The JRMSU System Library are into Interlibrary Loan to:



a. promote a good understanding of the Library's objectives and services among governing officials, civic leaders, and the general public;



b. promote active participation in the varied services offered by the library to people in different colleges.



Guest Researchers (refer to students, faculty and researchers from other school institution) except those with MOA may use the library materials upon payment of PhP50.00 for one (1) day use of the library and submission of the following to the circulation Section In- charge.



1. A valid school /office ID



2. A referral letter issued by the librarian or administrator of the institution
Audio-Visual Service

Under this section is instructional non-print materials such as television set, VHS, DVD, charts, and maps, slides, CDs, CD-ROMs, etc. These materials are to be used with the assistance of the in charge. A written request by the users is required to facilitate the scheduling of viewers to avoid overlapping. Thus, the use of multimedia section and equipment should be arranged or booked by the faculty one (1) week ahead of time to prevent conflict of schedule.
Electronic & Internet Services

This is a work place or computer terminal assigned to a researcher that serves as an access to a network and electronic resources. It only allows navigating the net.



A Public access to computers is available to clients on a first-come, first-served basis. Instructions for operating hardware are displayed near the computer. There is no charge for use of computers, however, in order to make the service available to as many clients as possible, a one (1) hour time limit for usage may is imposed specially during the peak hour demand. Library staff is available for general assistance in using the computer; however, staff are not expected to train clients individually in the use of application programs, instead, tutorial manuals will be provided when available and the library staff will schedule group orientation sessions.



Moreover, a provision of a free wifi connection is being added to the library services for the use of the clients who wish to go online via personal laptops, tablets, smart/android phones and other electronic gadgets.



On the other hand, the JRMSU System Library impose restrictions on duplication of copyrighted materials. Any violation of copyright law is the responsibility of the copy machine user.



Circulation (Check-in and out of Library Materials)

This is the section where borrowing and returning of books are done.


A. Loan periods/policies



1. Students



a. Lending period is for overnight only.

b. Generally, reference books, theses/dissertations and periodicals will not be checked out.

c. Borrowing of the same book may be renewed up to three times if there is no waiting list for the title. Renewal may be denied if other clients have placed reserve requests and are waiting for them.

d. Books with more than two copies of the same title shall be allowed for overnight use.

e. A student shall be allowed to borrow only one book at a time.

f. Borrowing of books for overnight use shall commence at 6:00 o’clock in the evening from Monday to Friday only. The books shall be returned between the hours of 7:00-9:00 o’clock in the morning of the following day. Failure to return the book on the specified time may subject the borrower to a penalty of P5.00.


2. Faculty


a. Regular faculty members may be allowed to borrow reference books up to a maximum of ten (10) titles for a period of one semester, which may be renewed for the next semester unless called for by another user. If, a borrower has an overdue book(s) he may not be allowed to borrow or renew a loan until the overdue book(s) is returned and the fine is paid. Only books with more than two (2) copies shall be allowed to be borrowed by the faculty.

b. Part-Time Instructors may borrow up to a maximum of three (3) titles for a period of one semester with his/her college dean’s approval as guarantor of the said references.

c. Administrative staff may be allowed to borrow two (2) circulation books at a time for two (2) weeks.



New way of borrowing and returning of borrowed materials is now done with the touch of electronic media through the integrated library system which the campus is adopting. This further executes extraction of reports, patron/user information, status of the material, inventory and many other features.
Current Awareness

The library provides and displays bulletin boards, current awareness bulletins, guides, pathfinders, signage, brochures, new acquisition list, specialized / selective bibliographies, handbook and other promotional materials.



This service further exemplifies the bulletin board of information trending the following thrusts:


1. Bulletin Board of Information to Different Colleges


2. Interactive Webpage of the JRMSU – TC Library


3. Newsletters, Flyers and Calendar of Activities
Library Orientation

Orientation with the use of the library during orientation program sponsored by the college administrators to all freshmen students is done every start of the semester. Classroom instruction is also put into practice upon the request for English or research methods class. Library orientation, personalized instruction for library research and tutorial in doing research via internet are also conducted.
